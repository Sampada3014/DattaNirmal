Credit scoring is a statistical method used by lenders (like banks and Financial Institutions ) to evaluate the creditworthiness of individuals or businesses. It's a way to predict how likely you are to repay borrowed money on time, based on your past credit behavior.

Here's a breakdown of how it works:

1. Information Gathering:

  • Infomerics Analytics Credit scoring Model collect data about your credit history. This information comes from various sources, including banks, credit card issuers, and other lenders.
  • This data includes things like your payment history, outstanding debts, types of credit accounts you have, the length of your credit history, and any new credit you've applied for.

2. Scoring Models:

  • Infomerics Analytics Credit scoring models uses its Analytics tool to analyze the information in your credit report to generate a three-digit score.
  • These models assign different weights to various factors based on how well they predict future credit behavior. For example, payment history is usually the most significant factor.

3.The Score:

Credit scores typically range from 300 to 850 (though some models might have slightly different ranges). In India, CIBIL scores range from 300 to 900.

A higher score generally indicates a lower credit risk, making it more likely that you'll be approved for credit and receive favorable terms like lower interest rates and higher credit limits.

  • Here's a general idea of score ranges and what they typically mean:
    • Excellent: 800-850 (or 750-900 in India)
    • Very Good: 740-799
    • Good: 670-739
    • Fair: 580-669
    • Poor: 300-579
